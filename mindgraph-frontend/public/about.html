About rawtxt

rawtxt is an AI-powered journaling tool built for people who think in text. You write freely, without structure or categories, and the product takes care of the rest: extracting the people, projects, deadlines, and behavioral patterns embedded in what you wrote, and building a living knowledge graph of your life over time.

The product is designed around a single conviction: the friction between having a thought and writing it down should be as close to zero as possible. No dropdowns. No tag selectors. No "choose a project" modal. If the AI can figure it out, it does. If it can't, it asks you exactly one question.

rawtxt runs an 8-node AI pipeline on every entry you submit: normalizing dates to your actual timezone, deduplicating near-identical submissions, extracting entities and their relationships, catching deadlines buried in casual language, and generating behavioral pattern insights across weeks of writing. The Ask Journal feature lets you query your own journal history through a conversational interface backed by hybrid retrieval (BM25 keyword search, vector similarity, and neural reranking).

The product is built and operated by a single person. It is not backed by venture capital and does not depend on advertising revenue. It is funded entirely by the people who find it useful enough to pay for.
