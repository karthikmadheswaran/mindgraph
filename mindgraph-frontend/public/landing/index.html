What it does

Eight ways your journal starts paying attention.

🧹

One Box. That's It.

You write. No titles, no tags, no "choose a project." MindGraph figures out what everything means. The less you have to think about the tool, the more you can think about your life.

🔍

Shiny Object Detector

Started a side project in February? Mentioned it 8 times and then went quiet? After 14 days of silence, MindGraph flags it. You decide if it's dead or just sleeping.

🧠

No Amnesia Mode

Ask about something you wrote six months ago. Hybrid search — vector similarity plus keyword plus a reranker — finds the right entry even when you can't remember the words you used.

📅

Commitment Catcher

Say "I need to finish this by Friday" in the middle of a rant. MindGraph pulls the deadline out — timezone-aware, calendar-grounded — and surfaces it on your dashboard before it slips.

🗺️

Your World, Mapped

Every person, project, and tool you mention gets linked into an interactive force-directed graph. See how everything connects. Click a node to see the entries behind it.

🪞

Patterns You Can't See Yourself

You consistently start projects on Mondays but your deep work happens Wednesday–Thursday. You mention headaches every time a specific project comes up. MindGraph finds these across weeks of entries, not just today's mood.

💬

Ask Your Past Self

"What was I thinking about Velora in March?" Conversational Q&A over your entire journal, with long-term memory compaction so it stays fast and accurate after thousands of entries.

📊

Your Dashboard, Auto-Built

Deadlines, active projects, and a timeline of what you actually finished this week — all assembled from the things you wrote, ranked by how often you mentioned them. No setup. No dragging cards between columns.
